Bridging the Public Health Information Gap

Health Sathi addresses the critical need for accessible and reliable health information in rural and semi-urban areas, breaking down barriers of language, distance, and misinformation.

Geographical Barriers

Overcoming limited access to physical healthcare facilities and trained professionals in remote regions.

Information Asymmetry

Providing verified health info in local languages to combat misinformation and panic.

Limited Infrastructure

Leveraging ubiquitous platforms like WhatsApp & SMS, accessible even on basic phones.

Government Health Schemes

Information about key health schemes from the Central and Odisha Governments.

Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB-PMJAY)

A flagship scheme providing a health cover of Rs. 5 lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization to over 12 crore poor and vulnerable families.

How to Apply:

Eligibility is based on the SECC 2011 database. No application is needed for listed families. Check eligibility on the official website or at an empanelled hospital.

Documents Required:

  • PMJAY e-card or a government-issued photo ID (like Aadhaar Card).
  • Ration Card to establish family structure.
Visit Official Site →

Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK)

Entitles all pregnant women delivering in public health institutions to absolutely free and no-expense delivery, including caesarean section, drugs, diet, and transport.

How to Apply:

Beneficiaries can avail of the services by visiting any government health facility (Sub-Centres, PHCs, CHCs, or District Hospitals) for their delivery and care.

Documents Required:

  • Mother and Child Protection (MCP) Card.
  • Aadhaar Card of the pregnant woman.
Visit Official Site →

Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)

An initiative for early identification and intervention for children from birth to 18 years to cover 4 ‘D’s viz. Defects at birth, Deficiencies, Diseases, and Development delays.

How to Apply:

Children are screened at Anganwadi Centres and Government schools by dedicated mobile health teams. If a condition is detected, referral and treatment are provided free of cost.

Documents Required:

  • Screening is done in batches; individual documents are usually not required initially.
  • Aadhaar and birth certificate may be required for treatment.
Visit Official Site →

Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA)

Provides pregnant ladies with a fixed day, free of cost, assured, and quality Antenatal Care on the 9th day of every month.

How to Apply:

Pregnant women can visit the nearest designated public health facility on the 9th of any month to avail services under this scheme.

Documents Required:

  • Mother and Child Protection (MCP) Card.
Visit Official Site →

Biju Swasthya Kalyan Yojana (BSKY)

A universal health coverage scheme providing cashless healthcare up to ₹5 lakh per family per annum (₹10 lakh for women) at empanelled private hospitals.

How to Apply:

All families with BSKY Smart Health Cards can avail the benefits. Card distribution is done by the government. Check eligibility with local health workers or on the official website.

Documents Required:

  • BSKY Smart Health Card is mandatory.
  • Any government-issued photo ID for verification at the hospital.
Visit Official Site →

NIDAN - Free Diagnostics Service

Provides free specified diagnostic services (pathology, digital X-ray, CT-Scan, MRI) in government health facilities, reducing out-of-pocket expenditure.

How to Apply:

Any patient with a doctor's prescription from a public health facility can avail these services for free, either in-house or through an outsourced agency.

Documents Required:

  • Doctor's prescription from the public health facility.
  • Patient's registration card from the hospital/CHC/PHC.
Learn More →

MAMATA Scheme

A conditional cash transfer maternity benefit scheme for pregnant and lactating women for the first two live births, providing monetary support to improve health and nutrition-seeking behaviours.

How to Apply:

Eligible women need to register their pregnancy at the Anganwadi Centre (AWC) within the first trimester. The ASHA worker facilitates the registration.

Documents Required:

  • Aadhaar Card.
  • Bank account details in the name of the beneficiary.
  • Mother and Child Protection (MCP) Card.
Visit Official Site →

Niramaya Yojana - Free Medicines

Ensures the availability of necessary medicines free of cost to all patients visiting public health facilities through a robust procurement and distribution system.

How to Apply:

Any patient who consults a doctor at a Government health facility is entitled to receive the prescribed medicines for free from the hospital's drug distribution counter (DDC).

Documents Required:

  • Doctor's prescription from the public health facility.
Visit Official Site →
